# Source Quality Hierarchy

When selecting which URLs to scrape, prioritize sources based on the topic type.

**Sections:** Technical | Company | Academic | Medical | Legal | Financial | News | Cross-Cutting Rules

## By Topic Type

### Technical / Engineering
1. Official documentation and specifications
2. Source code repositories (README, issues, changelogs)
3. Maintainer blog posts and release announcements
4. Conference talks and proceedings
5. In-depth technical blog posts from known practitioners
6. Stack Overflow answers with high votes and recent activity
7. General tech news coverage

### Company / Business
1. Company official website, press releases, investor relations
2. SEC filings, annual reports, earnings transcripts
3. Product documentation and pricing pages
4. Industry analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
5. Verified news coverage from business press (Reuters, Bloomberg, FT)
6. Customer reviews and case studies on third-party platforms
7. Founder/exec interviews and conference appearances

### Academic / Scientific

Use the hierarchy based on question type:

**For effectiveness, safety, consensus, or state-of-evidence questions:**
1. Systematic reviews and meta-analyses
2. High-quality guidelines or consensus statements
3. Peer-reviewed journal articles
4. Preprints (explicitly note preprint status)
5. Institutional reports (university, research lab, government agency)
6. Conference proceedings

**For frontier research, new findings, or mechanism questions:**
1. Peer-reviewed journal articles
2. Preprints (explicitly note preprint status)
3. Conference proceedings
4. Institutional reports
5. Science journalism from reputable outlets

### Medical / Health

**For diagnosis, treatment, or clinical recommendations:**
1. Clinical practice guidelines (WHO, NIH, NICE, specialty societies)
2. Systematic reviews and meta-analyses (Cochrane, etc.)
3. Regulatory agency publications (FDA, EMA)
4. Peer-reviewed clinical studies
5. Authoritative medical references (UpToDate, BMJ Best Practice)

**For emerging evidence or newly studied interventions:**
Individual studies may be necessary, but must be labeled as preliminary if consensus is not established.

6. Major medical institution patient education pages
7. Health journalism from outlets with medical review processes

### Legal / Policy / Regulatory
1. Primary law: statutes, regulations, treaties, court opinions
2. Regulatory agency guidance, advisories, enforcement actions
3. Official legislative history and committee reports
4. Legal scholarship (law review articles)
5. Reputable legal news and analysis (Law360, Reuters Legal)
6. Government agency FAQs and compliance guides
7. Bar association publications and practice guides

### Financial / Investment
1. Regulatory filings (SEC EDGAR, equivalent local regulators)
2. Central bank publications and data
3. Official economic data (BLS, Eurostat, World Bank)
4. Earnings calls and investor presentations
5. Reputable financial press (FT, WSJ, Bloomberg)
6. Industry association reports
7. Analyst reports from known firms

### News / Current Events
1. Original reporting from the outlet that broke the story
2. Official statements from parties involved
3. Wire services (AP, Reuters, AFP)
4. Cross-verified facts appearing in 3+ independent outlets
5. Follow-up investigative reporting
6. Expert commentary from domain specialists
7. Social media only as primary source (e.g., official account announcements)

## Cross-Cutting Rules

- **Prefer primary over secondary**: the original study over the blog post about it
- **Recency depends on topic**: recency matters most for news, policy, pricing, product behavior, and rapidly changing technical topics; older sources may still be primary for foundational background
- **Prefer institutional over individual**: unless the individual is the recognized authority
- **Match source requirements to claim type**: a single authoritative primary source is sufficient for direct factual claims; interpretive or disputed claims require multiple independent sources (see [claim-types.md](claim-types.md))
- **Distrust content farms and SEO bait**: thin content, keyword-stuffed titles, no author attribution
- **Note conflicts of interest**: vendor writing about own product, funded studies, affiliate content
